Experiment A.
At room temperature, a concentrated solution of hydrochloric acid gives off a gas called hydrogen chloride, HCl.
A concentrated solution of ammonium hydroxide gives off a gas called ammonia, NH3.
When hydrogen chloride and ammonia gas mix, one of the products is a white solid called ammonium chloride, NH4Cl.

In the laboratory, a glass tube is open at both ends.
A wad of cotton soaked in concentrated hydrochloric acid is inserted into the right-hand opening.
A similar wad soaked in concentrated ammonium hydroxide is inserted in the left-hand opening.

After a few minutes the experimenter notes that a white ring is formed near the center of the tube.

Experiment B.

Hydrogen sulfide is a colorless gas, slightly heavier than air, with a very strong odor of rotten eggs.
A small stoppered bottle of hydrogen sulfide is placed in a corner of the laboratory.
The stopper is removed, and a few seconds later the odor of rotten eggs is detectable in all parts of the laboratory.
