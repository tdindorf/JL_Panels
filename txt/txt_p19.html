Each of these figures represents a container in which a gas is confined.

  • Fig. 1 is a container with a fixed volume.
  • Fig. 2 and 3 have a movable piston, so that the gas expands or is compressed at constant pressure.
  • The containers in Figs. 1 and 2 are made of a material through which heat passes quite readily i.e. heat conductors.
  • In Fig. 3, the container and piston are made of materials called heat insulators, through which heat transfers negligibly.
