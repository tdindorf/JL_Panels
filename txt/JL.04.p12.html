We can now summarize the assumptions made so far:
(the number and order are not important; different sources sometimes combine these statements into fewer, sometimes split thim into more numerous ones)

    The first four assumptions are part of a more general kinetic theory of matter
  1. Matter consists of particles; the total mass of these particle is the mass of the substance/object.
  2. These particles are in constant, random, and rapid motion, as a result of which they collide among themselves and (in case of a fluid) with the walls of the container.
  3. All these collisions are perfectly elastic.
  4. The average kinetic energy of the particles depends only on the absolute temperature of the system.

    5. The next assumptions are applicable to gases only, and complete the kinetic theory of gases:
  5. In a gas, the total volume of the individual particles is negligible compared to the volume occupied by the gas (= the average distance separating the gas particles is large compared to their size);
  6. Except during collisions, the interactions among molecules are negligible (= they exert no long-range (a.k.a. inter-molecular) forces on one another);
  7. The duration of individual collisions is negligible when compared to the time between successive collisions.

(One can argue about whether (4) is an assumption or a deduction)
